# Manager Track — PM Career Framework (August 2023)

> Reference document for the Product Coach skill. Contains the complete Manager leveling matrix from Spotify's PM Career Framework.

---

## Level Overview

| Level | Title | Workday Level | Short Description | Business Need |
|-------|-------|---------------|-------------------|---------------|
| 4 | Senior Product Manager | Level H | A new manager developing their skills as a PM leader | Recommended given org implications |
| 5 | Group Product Manager | Level G | An experienced manager leading the PM team for an organization | Required (organizational need) |
| 6 | Director | Level F | A highly-experienced leader of a PM team for a large organization including PM managers | Required (organizational need) |
| 7 | Senior Director | Level E | A highly-experienced leader of the product discipline for the largest organizations | Required (organizational need) |

---

## Differences from Expert Track

The Manager Track shares 9 of its 12 criteria with the Expert (IC) Track. Three criteria are unique to managers, replacing the IC-only criteria that measure individual execution depth.

**Shared criteria (9):**

1. Portfolio size & complexity
2. Roadmap horizon
3. Ambiguity
4. Roadmapping
5. Trade-offs
6. Trust & influence
7. Communication
8. Community & mentorship
9. Scale of impact

**Manager-specific criteria (3):**

6. Team size
7. People growth
8. Organization health

These three criteria replace the IC-specific expectations around personal execution depth and hands-on delivery. Where the Expert Track measures how deeply an individual PM can go, the Manager Track measures how effectively a PM leader builds, grows, and sustains a team of PMs.

Key structural difference: the Manager Track starts at Level 4 (Senior PM), not Level 1. There is no manager equivalent for Associate PM, PM I, or PM II. Entry to the Manager Track requires Senior PM caliber product skills plus demonstrated readiness to lead people.

## 1. Portfolio size & complexity

**Category:** Product Scope — Achievement
**Track:** Shared with Expert Track

| Senior PM (mgr) — Level H | Group PM — Level G | Director — Level F | Senior Director — Level E |
|---|---|---|---|
| Responsible for a complex product with several configurations, features, functions and flows that produce a distinct outcome. Accountable for a portfolio of 2-3 PMs. | Accountable for the portfolio of a PM team, often a few products within a product line | Accountable for the portfolio of a large product team, often an entire product line | Accountable for the portfolio of very a large product team, often multiple product or business lines |

### In artifacts, look for

- Number of products, product areas, or distinct problem spaces in scope
- Evidence of managing dependencies across multiple product surfaces
- References to team size and how portfolio is distributed across PMs
- Org charts or team structure diagrams showing PM accountability boundaries
- Quarterly or annual planning docs that allocate capacity across products
- Stakeholder maps showing breadth of product surface area

### Calibration helper

**Senior PM (mgr) — Level H**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Owns a single product or feature area with no PM reports; portfolio is essentially an IC scope with a title change | Accountable for a complex product with clear PM delegation across 2-3 reports; can articulate how work is distributed and why | Managing portfolio breadth comparable to a Group PM; overseeing multiple distinct products or a full product line across more than 3 PMs |

**Group PM — Level G**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Portfolio is limited to a single product with a small team; not yet managing across multiple product areas | Accountable for multiple products within a product line; PM team covers distinct problem spaces with clear ownership boundaries | Portfolio spans an entire product line or crosses product lines; scope resembles a Director-level remit |

**Director — Level F**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Portfolio covers a few products but not a full product line; team size or scope is closer to Group PM | Accountable for an entire product line with a large PM team; portfolio includes both execution and strategic products | Portfolio spans multiple product lines or business units; scope and organizational complexity resemble Senior Director |

**Senior Director — Level E**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Portfolio is a single product line, even if the team is large; not yet spanning multiple business areas | Accountable for multiple product or business lines with a very large PM team; portfolio decisions affect company-level outcomes | N/A — this is the top of the manager track |

## 2. Roadmap horizon

**Category:** Product Scope — Achievement
**Track:** Shared with Expert Track

| Senior PM (mgr) — Level H | Group PM — Level G | Director — Level F | Senior Director — Level E |
|---|---|---|---|
| Works towards bi-annual and annual objectives. | Works towards bi-annual and annual objectives. | Works towards bi-annual, annual and multi-year objectives. | Works towards bi-annual, annual and multi-year objectives. |

### In artifacts, look for

- Planning documents with explicit time horizons (H1/H2, annual, multi-year)
- Roadmaps that extend beyond the current quarter
- Strategy documents with phased investment or sequencing over multiple periods
- References to long-term bets vs. near-term commitments
- OKR or goal-setting docs that span bi-annual or annual cycles
- Multi-year vision documents or technology investment plans

### Calibration helper

**Senior PM (mgr) — Level H**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Planning is quarter-by-quarter with no articulated half-year or annual arc; roadmaps are reactive | Plans and sequences work across bi-annual and annual horizons; can connect quarterly execution to annual objectives | Actively shaping multi-year strategy alongside annual plans; thinking beyond the 12-month window |

**Group PM — Level G**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Roadmap rarely extends beyond the current half; annual planning is nominal | Plans across bi-annual and annual cycles; sequences work to achieve organization-level annual objectives | Driving multi-year strategic planning; roadmap includes explicit 2-3 year investment theses |

**Director — Level F**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Planning stops at the annual level; no evidence of multi-year thinking or investment sequencing | Plans across bi-annual, annual, and multi-year horizons; roadmap includes phased bets with long-term payoffs | Multi-year planning spans company-level strategy; shaping investment decisions across business lines |

**Senior Director — Level E**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Multi-year planning is aspirational but not operationalized; annual execution dominates | Plans across all horizons; multi-year roadmap directly shapes company-level investment and strategy | N/A — this is the top of the manager track |

## 3. Ambiguity

**Category:** Product Strategy — Competence
**Track:** Shared with Expert Track

| Senior PM (mgr) — Level H | Group PM — Level G | Director — Level F | Senior Director — Level E |
|---|---|---|---|
| Given a broader problem with strategic ambiguity, can break it down into multiple problems and prioritize them for discovery | Given a very large and unexplored problem with high strategic ambiguity, can break it down into multiple problems and prioritize them for teams to do discovery | Given a very large and unexplored problem with no strategic guidance, can break it down into multiple problems and prioritize them for teams to do discovery | Given a very large and unexplored problem with no strategic guidance, can break it down into multiple problems and prioritize them for teams to do discovery |

### In artifacts, look for

- Problem frames that decompose a broad or ambiguous mandate into specific sub-problems
- Prioritization frameworks applied to competing discovery areas
- Evidence of navigating situations where leadership did not prescribe the approach
- Documents that structure exploration (hypotheses, learning plans, kill criteria)
- Decision logs showing how ambiguity was resolved through sequenced learning
- Delegation of discovery to specific PMs or squads with clear problem assignments

### Calibration helper

**Senior PM (mgr) — Level H**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Struggles to decompose broad problems; waits for clearer direction or works on only the most obvious sub-problem | Takes a strategically ambiguous problem, breaks it into prioritized sub-problems, and sequences discovery across them | Navigates high ambiguity with no strategic guidance; independently structures exploration for multiple teams |

**Group PM — Level G**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Can decompose problems but relies on strategic guidance from leadership to prioritize; discovery delegation is unclear | Takes very large, unexplored problems with high strategic ambiguity and structures discovery across multiple teams with clear prioritization | Operates with no strategic guidance at all; creates the strategic framing from scratch for the largest organizational problems |

**Director — Level F**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Needs some strategic framing or leadership input before structuring exploration; delegation is effective but reactive | Takes very large, unexplored problems with no strategic guidance and independently structures and prioritizes discovery across teams | Shapes company-level strategic framing in areas with no precedent; defines new problem categories |

**Senior Director — Level E**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Effective in ambiguity but within a single product line; not yet driving cross-business problem structuring | Structures exploration for the largest, most ambiguous problems across multiple business lines with no guidance | N/A — this is the top of the manager track |

## 4. Roadmapping

**Category:** Product Strategy — Competence
**Track:** Shared with Expert Track

| Senior PM (mgr) — Level H | Group PM — Level G | Director — Level F | Senior Director — Level E |
|---|---|---|---|
| Produces actionable and strategically sequenced roadmaps for a complex product or portfolio of multiple products | Strategically sequences work within a 6-12 month time period for their scope | Strategically sequences work within a 6-12 month time period for their scope | Strategically sequences work within a 6-12 month time period for their scope |

### In artifacts, look for

- Roadmap documents with explicit sequencing rationale (why this order)
- Evidence of strategic prioritization rather than stakeholder-driven ordering
- Capacity allocation across products or teams within the portfolio
- Dependency mapping across squads or product areas
- Roadmaps that connect near-term execution to longer-term strategic objectives
- Quarterly or half-year planning decks showing phased delivery

### Calibration helper

**Senior PM (mgr) — Level H**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Roadmap is a feature list ordered by urgency; no strategic sequencing or cross-product coordination | Produces roadmaps with clear strategic sequencing across a complex product or multi-product portfolio; dependencies are explicit | Roadmaps span multiple product areas with sophisticated sequencing; resembles Group PM-level strategic coordination |

**Group PM — Level G**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Sequencing is limited to a single product; roadmap does not reflect organization-level strategy | Strategically sequences work across their full scope within 6-12 month windows; roadmap reflects organization priorities | Roadmap coordination extends beyond their scope; influencing cross-organization sequencing |

**Director — Level F**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Sequencing is effective within parts of the product line but not the full scope; gaps in cross-team coordination | Strategically sequences work across an entire product line within 6-12 month windows; roadmap integrates inputs from multiple teams | Roadmap sequencing influences company-level priorities; coordination extends across studios |

**Senior Director — Level E**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Roadmap coordination covers a product line but does not extend to cross-business sequencing | Strategically sequences work across multiple product or business lines; roadmap decisions shape company-level resource allocation | N/A — this is the top of the manager track |

## 5. Trade-offs

**Category:** Product Strategy — Competence
**Track:** Shared with Expert Track

| Senior PM (mgr) — Level H | Group PM — Level G | Director — Level F | Senior Director — Level E |
|---|---|---|---|
| Makes trade-offs between multiple team objectives that can impact the organization's goals | Resolves very complex trade-offs between multiple organization objectives | Resolves trade-offs with the highest degree of complexity between objectives that can impact the company | Resolves trade-offs with the highest degree of complexity between objectives that can impact the company |

### In artifacts, look for

- Decision documents with explicit trade-off analysis (what was chosen, what was deferred, why)
- Evidence of navigating conflicting team or organization objectives
- Capacity allocation decisions that required deprioritizing one objective for another
- Stakeholder alignment documents for contentious trade-offs
- Post-mortems or retrospectives referencing trade-off decisions and their outcomes
- Trade-off frameworks or principles used to resolve recurring tensions

### Calibration helper

**Senior PM (mgr) — Level H**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Avoids trade-offs or escalates them; decisions stay within a single team's objectives without considering organizational impact | Makes trade-offs between multiple team objectives with clear rationale; understands and communicates impact on organization goals | Resolves organization-level trade-offs independently; navigates multi-stakeholder conflicts comparable to Group PM scope |

**Group PM — Level G**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Trade-offs are team-scoped; struggles to navigate conflicting organization-level objectives | Resolves very complex trade-offs between multiple organization objectives; stakeholders trust the process and outcomes | Navigates company-impacting trade-offs; resolution complexity approaches Director level |

**Director — Level F**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Trade-offs stay within the organization; does not yet navigate company-level conflicts | Resolves the highest-complexity trade-offs between objectives that impact the company; manages political and strategic dimensions | Trade-off resolution shapes company strategy; influences how the company makes decisions at scale |

**Senior Director — Level E**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Trade-offs are high complexity but scoped to a single product line or studio | Resolves the highest-complexity trade-offs that impact the company; decisions set precedent for how the organization navigates tension | N/A — this is the top of the manager track |

## 6. Team size

**Category:** Leadership — Behavior
**Track:** Manager-specific (not on Expert Track)

| Senior PM (mgr) — Level H | Group PM — Level G | Director — Level F | Senior Director — Level E |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hires, retains and grows a team of 2-3 PMs | Hires, retains and grows a team of 4 to 7 PMs | Hires, retains and grows a team of managers and PMs (8-20 total size) | Hires, retains and grows a team of leaders, managers and PMs (20+ total size) |

### In artifacts, look for

- Hiring plans and job descriptions drafted or approved by the manager
- Team structure documents showing reporting lines and PM-to-product mapping
- Retention strategies or career conversations documented in 1:1 notes
- Onboarding plans for new PM hires
- Org design proposals showing how the PM team scales with product scope
- Headcount requests with business justification
- Evidence of succession planning for key PM roles
- Interview panel participation and hiring process ownership

### Calibration helper

**Senior PM (mgr) — Level H**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Has 0-1 reports; functioning as an IC with a management title; no evidence of hiring or team-building activity | Actively hires, retains, and grows a team of 2-3 PMs; has demonstrated at least one successful hire and one retention or growth action | Managing 4+ PMs with evidence of team design decisions; operating at Group PM team scale |

**Group PM — Level G**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Team is 2-3 PMs; not yet managing at the scale expected for this level | Hires, retains, and grows a team of 4-7 PMs; evidence of deliberate team composition and role design | Managing 8+ PMs including other managers; team structure complexity approaches Director level |

**Director — Level F**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Team is fewer than 8; not yet managing managers or operating at product-line scale | Hires, retains, and grows a team of managers and PMs (8-20 total); evidence of managing managers and designing org structure | Team exceeds 20; managing leaders and setting organizational direction at Senior Director scale |

**Senior Director — Level E**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Team is under 20; not yet managing at multi-leader, multi-layer scale | Hires, retains, and grows a team of leaders, managers, and PMs (20+ total); evidence of scaling the PM organization itself | N/A — this is the top of the manager track |

## 7. People growth

**Category:** Leadership — Behavior
**Track:** Manager-specific (not on Expert Track)

| Senior PM (mgr) — Level H | Group PM — Level G | Director — Level F | Senior Director — Level E |
|---|---|---|---|
| Learns about the Spotify Performance Development model and makes sure their directs have the support they need. | Applies Spotify Performance Development model resulting growth of direct reports in line with business needs | Grows new leaders and coaches them on how to manage challenges and cultivate talent | Grows new leaders and coaches them on how to manage challenges and cultivate talent |

### In artifacts, look for

- Development plans for direct reports with specific growth goals
- Evidence of using the Spotify Performance Development model (check-ins, calibration, feedback)
- Promotion narratives or cases written by the manager for their reports
- Coaching notes or 1:1 artifacts showing growth-oriented conversations
- Evidence of stretch assignments or deliberate problem allocation for growth
- Track record of PMs who grew under this manager (promotions, expanded scope)
- Manager readiness assessments for reports transitioning to people management
- Talent review or calibration participation artifacts

### Calibration helper

**Senior PM (mgr) — Level H**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| No evidence of engaging with the Performance Development model; directs lack development plans or structured support | Learning and applying the Performance Development model; directs have development plans and receive regular support | Actively growing PMs with measurable outcomes (promotions, scope expansions); applying the model with sophistication beyond a new manager |

**Group PM — Level G**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Understands the model but application is inconsistent; direct report growth is not clearly tied to business needs | Applies the Performance Development model consistently; direct reports are growing in line with business needs; evidence of at least one successful promotion or scope expansion | Growing new managers; coaching reports through people management challenges; functioning as a talent incubator |

**Director — Level F**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Growing individual PMs but not yet developing new managers or leaders | Grows new leaders; coaches managers on talent cultivation and managing challenges; track record of producing managers | Developing senior leaders; shaping how the organization approaches talent development at scale |

**Senior Director — Level E**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Developing managers but not yet growing senior leaders or shaping discipline-wide talent strategy | Grows new leaders at all levels; coaches on the hardest management challenges; talent development is a recognized organizational strength | N/A — this is the top of the manager track |

## 8. Organization health

**Category:** Leadership — Behavior
**Track:** Manager-specific (not on Expert Track)

| Senior PM (mgr) — Level H | Group PM — Level G | Director — Level F | Senior Director — Level E |
|---|---|---|---|
| Learns how to effectively assign problems across autonomous PMs in an open and safe environment for the team. | Identifies anti-patterns that impact org health and proposes or leads changes to improve it. | Drives product organization changes to optimise for the future, in terms of delivery and team health. | Drives product organization changes to optimise for the future, in terms of delivery and team health. |

### In artifacts, look for

- Problem assignment documents showing how work is distributed across PMs
- Team health survey results or retrospective summaries
- Reorg proposals or team structure change documents with rationale
- Evidence of psychological safety initiatives (open feedback loops, blameless retrospectives)
- Anti-pattern identification and remediation plans
- Org design documents that optimize for delivery and team health simultaneously
- Operating model documentation (how the PM team works together)
- Escalation frameworks or decision-rights documents
- Evidence of adjusting team structure based on delivery outcomes or health signals

### Calibration helper

**Senior PM (mgr) — Level H**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Problem assignment is unclear or defaults to availability; team dynamics are not actively managed | Learning to assign problems effectively across autonomous PMs; creating an open, safe environment; evidence of intentional problem distribution | Identifying and addressing org health anti-patterns proactively; proposing structural changes beyond basic problem assignment |

**Group PM — Level G**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Aware of org health issues but not yet diagnosing anti-patterns or proposing structural changes | Identifies anti-patterns impacting org health; proposes or leads changes to improve it; evidence of diagnosing and resolving at least one structural problem | Driving proactive org changes to optimize for the future; operating at Director-level org design sophistication |

**Director — Level F**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Reacts to org health problems but does not proactively redesign for the future; changes are incremental | Drives product organization changes to optimize for delivery and team health; evidence of proactive restructuring with measurable outcomes | Shaping org design principles beyond their own team; influencing how the company thinks about PM organization |

**Senior Director — Level E**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Org changes are effective within a single product line but not yet shaping cross-organization structure | Drives organization changes across multiple teams to optimize for the future; org design decisions affect the broader PM discipline | N/A — this is the top of the manager track |

**Director — Level F**

## 10. Communication

**Category:** Leadership — Behavior
**Track:** Shared with Expert Track

| Senior PM (mgr) — Level H | Group PM — Level G | Director — Level F | Senior Director — Level E |
|---|---|---|---|
| Effective in communicating context, vision, and strategy to a group of squads and stakeholders | Effective in communicating context, vision and strategy to an organization, program participants and stakeholders | Effective in communicating context, vision and strategy to program participants, stakeholders, and the company | Effective in communicating context, vision, and strategy to program participants, stakeholders, and the company |

### In artifacts, look for

- Strategy narratives or vision documents shared with squads and stakeholders
- Town hall or all-hands presentations
- Written updates that communicate direction and context (not just status)
- Evidence of audience-appropriate communication (different artifacts for engineers vs. leadership)
- Slack posts, email updates, or memos that set strategic context

- Evidence of creating shared understanding across organizational boundaries

|---|---|---|

**Group PM — Level G**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Communication is effective within a few squads but does not reach the full organization or program participants | Effectively communicates context, vision, and strategy to an organization, program participants, and stakeholders | Communication influences company-level narrative; presentations and documents are referenced beyond the organization |

**Director — Level F**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Communication reaches the organization but does not effectively land at the company level | Effectively communicates context, vision, and strategy to program participants, stakeholders, and the company | Communication shapes company-level strategy discussions; recognized as a voice in company-wide product narrative |

**Senior Director — Level E**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|

## 12. Scale of impact

**Category:** Impact — Achievement
**Track:** Shared with Expert Track

| Senior PM (mgr) — Level H | Group PM — Level G | Director — Level F | Senior Director — Level E |
|---|---|---|---|
| Track record of delivering sustainable and high-value/performing products for internal or external customers across multiple complex products that meets the team's objectives | Consistent delivery of value that achieves objectives for an organization | Consistent delivery of value that achieves objectives for a studio | Consistent delivery of value that achieves objectives for a large studio |

### In artifacts, look for

- Evidence of sustained product delivery (not one-off launches)
- Metrics showing product performance against team, org, or studio objectives
- Portfolio-level outcome summaries (not just individual product results)
- OKR achievement records across multiple quarters
- Business impact narratives connecting product work to organizational outcomes
- Evidence of value delivery breadth (across multiple products, not just one)
- Studio-level or company-level impact statements with supporting data

### Calibration helper

**Senior PM (mgr) — Level H**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Impact is limited to a single product; no track record across multiple complex products | Track record of delivering sustainable, high-value products across multiple complex products that meet team objectives | Impact consistently achieves organization-level objectives; delivery scope and consistency resemble Group PM |

**Group PM — Level G**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Delivers value but inconsistently or within a narrow scope; objectives are team-level, not organization-level | Consistent delivery of value that achieves objectives for an organization; track record spans multiple quarters | Impact achieves studio-level objectives; delivery consistency and scale approach Director level |

**Director — Level F**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Delivers value for an organization but not yet at studio-level impact | Consistent delivery of value that achieves objectives for a studio; impact is recognized at the studio level | Impact achieves objectives for a large studio or spans multiple studios; approaching Senior Director scale |

**Senior Director — Level E**

| Below Level | At Level | Above Level |
|---|---|---|
| Impact is studio-scoped but not yet achieving objectives for a large studio or across studios | Consistent delivery of value that achieves objectives for a large studio; impact shapes business-level outcomes | N/A — this is the top of the manager track |
