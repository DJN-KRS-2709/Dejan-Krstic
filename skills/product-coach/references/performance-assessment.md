# Performance Assessment — WHAT + HOW Framework

> Reference document for the Product Coach skill. Used in `--self-review` mode to overlay Spotify's performance model on career framework evaluation.

## WHAT — Outcome Questions

The WHAT dimension measures **results delivered**. When evaluating a PM's body of work, assess against these 5 questions:

| # | Question | What to look for |
|---|----------|-----------------|
| 1 | Did you deliver on your committed goals? | Goal completion rate, scope changes documented, reasons for misses |
| 2 | Did you deliver impact beyond your immediate scope? | Cross-team contributions, unplanned wins, initiative beyond role |
| 3 | Did you deliver sustainable outcomes? | Long-term health of shipped products, tech debt created vs. resolved, operational burden |
| 4 | Did you deliver outcomes aligned with company priorities? | Connection to company bets, strategic alignment, mission-level impact |
| 5 | Did you deliver outcomes efficiently? | Time-to-value, resource utilization, opportunity cost of what was NOT done |

### WHAT Evaluation in Artifacts

When scanning a PM's body of work for WHAT signals:

- **Status updates**: Look for goal tracking, milestone completion, pivot documentation
- **Evidence files**: Look for metrics, before/after comparisons, user impact data
- **Decision logs**: Look for decisions that unblocked progress, prevented waste
- **PRDs**: Look for clear success metrics, measurable outcomes, connection to objectives

## HOW — Values in Action

The HOW dimension measures **behaviors demonstrated**. Spotify's 9 Values in Action are grouped into 3 categories:

### Innovative (How we create)

| Value | Description | Artifact signals |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| Bold | Takes calculated risks, challenges the status quo | Novel approaches in PRDs, willingness to kill ideas, non-obvious problem framing |
| Genuine | Authentic, transparent about uncertainties | Honest status updates, explicit unknowns, confidence signaling (confirmed/reported/inferred) |
| Collaborative | Works across boundaries, seeks diverse input | Cross-team stakeholder inclusion, XFN alignment evidence, shared decision-making |

### Sincere (How we relate)

| Value | Description | Artifact signals |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| Respectful | Values diverse perspectives, inclusive | Multiple viewpoints in decision docs, stakeholder voice representation |
| Caring | Considers impact on people, empathetic | User research integration, creator/partner impact consideration, team health awareness |
| Playful | Brings energy, finds joy in work | Creative problem framing, engaging communication style, experimentation mindset |

### Passionate (How we deliver)

| Value | Description | Artifact signals |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| Driven | Persistent, outcome-oriented | Follow-through on decisions, iteration evidence, completion rate |
| Resourceful | Finds creative solutions with constraints | Working within constraints, creative scoping, leveraging existing tools |
| Focused | Prioritizes ruthlessly, says no | Clear non-goals, explicit trade-offs, scope containment evidence |

## Impact Scale

The Impact Scale determines the overall performance rating. It maps outcomes (WHAT) and behaviors (HOW) to a 5-tier scale:

| Tier | Label | Distribution | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | Behind | ~5% | Not meeting expectations for current level |
| 2 | Developing | ~15% | Partially meeting expectations, growth areas identified |
| 3 | Performing | ~60% | Consistently meeting expectations for current level |
| 4 | Exceeding | ~15% | Consistently exceeding expectations, demonstrating next-level signals |
| 5 | Outstanding | ~5% | Exceptional impact well beyond current level, clear promotion signal |

### Rating Matrix

|  | HOW: Below | HOW: At | HOW: Above |
|--|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **WHAT: Below** | Behind (1) | Developing (2) | Developing (2) |
| **WHAT: At** | Developing (2) | Performing (3) | Exceeding (4) |
| **WHAT: Above** | Performing (3) | Exceeding (4) | Outstanding (5) |

### Calibration notes

- WHAT and HOW are weighted equally — strong outcomes with poor behaviors (or vice versa) cap the rating
- "Performing" (Tier 3) is a strong rating — it means the PM is doing their job well at level
- "Exceeding" (Tier 4) should show consistent next-level evidence, not one-off spikes
- "Outstanding" (Tier 5) is rare and requires sustained, exceptional impact that others recognize

## 4 Differentiation Questions

These questions help distinguish between levels and identify promotion readiness:

| # | Question | Purpose | How to assess |
|---|----------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | **Scope:** Is this PM operating at the scope expected for their level? | Validates level placement | Compare portfolio complexity against framework expectations |
| 2 | **Consistency:** Is this PM demonstrating these capabilities consistently, not just in peak moments? | Separates sustained performance from spikes | Look across multiple artifacts, not just the best one |
| 3 | **Independence:** How much direction does this PM need? | Key differentiator between adjacent levels | Check for self-directed discovery, proactive problem identification |
| 4 | **Multiplier effect:** Is this PM making others around them more effective? | Distinguishes senior from mid-level | Look for mentoring, process improvements, XFN enablement |

### Using Differentiation Questions in Self-Review

When running `--self-review`, apply these questions after the per-criterion evaluation:

1. **For each criterion rated "At Level"**: Ask — is this consistent across the review period, or based on one artifact?
2. **For each criterion rated "Above Level"**: Ask — could this PM do this independently, or did they need significant guidance?
3. **For the overall assessment**: Ask — is this PM making their team/org better, or just delivering individually?
4. **For promotion readiness**: All 4 differentiation questions must be answered affirmatively at the NEXT level for a strong promotion case.

## Self-Review Output Structure

When generating a self-review, structure the output as:

1. **WHAT Assessment** — Goal delivery, impact scope, sustainability, alignment, efficiency
2. **HOW Assessment** — Per-value signals from artifacts (Innovative, Sincere, Passionate)
3. **Impact Scale Placement** — Tier assignment with evidence
4. **Differentiation Check** — 4 questions answered with evidence
5. **Gap-to-Next-Level** — What's needed for promotion, organized by criterion
6. **30-Day Focus** — The single highest-leverage action to close the biggest gap
