## The Enablement Test

Every piece of work can be tested against one question:

**"What can Finance or the business now do — better, faster, more reliably, or without manual effort — because of this work?"**

## Impact = WHAT x HOW

- **WHAT** — The outcomes your work created
- **HOW** — The values you demonstrated delivering them

### Impact Scale

Low | Inconsistent | Consistent | High | Exceptional

## Six Categories of FinE Impact

1. **Delivery** — Shipping committed work on time, at quality
   - *Counterfactual: If we'd missed this deadline, what business process would have stalled?*

2. **Operational Excellence** — Systems running, incidents handled, close cycles supported
   - *Counterfactual: If this system had gone down during close, what would Finance have had to do manually?*

3. **Enabling** — Unblocking others, platform work, APIs
   - *Counterfactual: If this capability didn't exist, how many teams would be building their own version?*

4. **Risk Reduction** — Compliance, audit, controls, error prevention
   - *Counterfactual: If we hadn't built this control, what exposure would Spotify carry?*

5. **Strategic Investment** — Tech debt, architecture, migrations
   - *Counterfactual: If we hadn't made this investment now, what would the next project have cost?*

6. **Automation & AI Readiness** — Making systems machine-consumable, agent-ready, and auditable
   - *Counterfactual: If we hadn't structured this for machine consumption, how long would the manual process have persisted?*

## Five Strategy Circles

- **Data** — More trustworthy, accessible, timely financial data?
- **Monetization Speed** — Faster to understand financial impact of business decisions?
- **AI Compatibility** — Financial workflows more machine-ready?
- **FinE Platform** — Reduced fragmentation, enabled reuse?
- **People & Flow** — FinE more flexible and resilient?

## The Impact Value Chain

Strategy Circle → Domain Outcome → Team Priority → Role Contribution → Impact Evidence

The chain breaks when any link is missing. Most common gaps:
- Teams don't know WHY their work matters to Finance
- People do good work but can't articulate its value

## Values in Action in FinE

- **Ownership (Make It Happen)** — Own the Finance outcome, not just the engineering artifact
- **Transparency (One Team)** — Surprises are expensive. Surface risks early. Make invisible work visible.
- **Collaboration (One Team)** — Make Finance partners more effective, not create friction
- **Human Judgement** — Balance speed with accuracy. Know when to ask. Reduce complexity.

## For rhythm skills

Skills that produce output (epic updates, sprint summaries, retro findings) should:
- Frame progress using the Enablement Test ("Finance can now...")
- Categorize impact (which of the 6 categories?)
- Use counterfactuals for risk and investment work
- Connect to strategy circles when possible
- Surface WHO drove the impact (the engineer-impact-mirror concept)

## Manager action (before April 3)

Run a 30-minute team conversation:
1. What outcomes does our team exist to deliver, and for whom?
2. Where does our impact show up? Which of the 6 categories?
3. Counterfactual exercise on a recent piece of work
